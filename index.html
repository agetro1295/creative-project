Why visit the Philippines

Be amazed at the white sand beaches and clear blue waters at Boracay Beach. It's a literal tropical paradise that simply must be visited.

Considered to be the worlds most perfectly formed volcano in terms of symmetry, the Mayon volcano is a dream country for those who love to hike and admire the Earth's natural wonders.

With towering limestone cliffs, underground rivers, encahting lagoons and rich coral gardens, El Nido in Palawan is the defintion of a tropical get-away.

The Banaue rice terraces consist of terraces that were handcarved into a mountain side by indigenous groups thousands of years ago. For those who have a knack for anthropology, this is a must-see attraction.

The Tubbataha Reef is a nationally protected reef that is made up of two atolls. The reef is known for its coral walls and diverse marine life which makes ideal for diving.

Coron is known as the shipwreck diving capital of the Philippines, if not the World and the town itself is quaint, cute and a haven for visitors looking to get their diving fix.It also has some of the bluest waters in the world with rock formations which pierce the surface and penetrate the skies.
